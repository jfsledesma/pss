Reticulated Python

Image courtesy of pixabay

Local name: 'Sawa'
Classification: Endangered
Endemic: No
Range: South-Southeast Asia

The reticulated python is the largest snake in the world in terms of length, contested only by the anaconda of South America and the rock pythons of Africa. While potentially dangerous, they are not as deadly as venomous snakes. Still, they are one of the snake species who are known to prey on humans.

Hunting as well as thriving demand in the pet trade for these animals - due to their beautiful exotic color possibilities - reticulated python populations have declined and the species has been classified as "Endangered."

Philippine Cobra

Image courtesy of Science Daily

Local name: 'Ulopong'
Classification: Near Threatened
Endemic: Yes
Range: Northern Luzon

The Philippine cobra is famously said to have one of the most potent venom in the world. Their venom is a powerful neurotoxin that affects the respiratory system, and a bite is potentially deadly to humans.

Like other cobras, the Philippine cobra is able to form a hood when threatened. It also possesses the ability to spit venom up to three meters away.

Samar Cobra

Image courtesy of Pestpedia

Local name: 'Agawason'
Classification: Least Concern
Endemic: Yes
Range: Visayas and Mindanao

The Samar Cobra is close relative to the Philippine cobra. Like it's cousin, it possesses strong venom and the ability to spit a considerable distance. The main difference is in the coloration and range, with the Philippine cobra found in the northern areas of the country and the Samar cobra being found in the southern islands. Although technically two different species of snake, they are similar enough that both are referred to as cobras.

Red-tailed Racer

Image courtesy of Pixabay

Classification: Least Concern
Endemic: No
Range: Southeast Asia

A relatively harmless, if not snappy, snake, the Red-tailed Racer (or Red-tailed Green Rat Snake) is often encountered and killed on sight due to how easy it is to mistake from the deadlier Pit Viper at a distance. Unlike a viper, the venom of a rat snake is harmless to humans, while populations of the animal are beneficial to keep around particularly near farmlands to control the population of mice.

Pit Vipers

Image courtesy of Pixabay

Classification: Diverse
Endemic: No
Range: ~

Pit vipers in the Philippines encompass a wide-range of species with several unifying qualities. They are considered hazards in places like agriculture fields due to their tendency to hide in grass, bushes, or tree branches and striking unaware workers. They have triangular heads which allow them to store and deliver more venom in a single bite. While their venom may not be the most potent among snake species, they are still capable of delivering a fatal bite.

Banded Krait

Image courtesy of Wikipedia

Classification: Least Concern
Endemic: No
Range: Oceans

Banded kraits are a common sight in Philippine salt water. Although their venom is potentially deadly, they are very shy and normally prefer to swim away rather than bite a person. Incidents involving this snake usually involve fishnets, which cause the snake to panic when it finds itself trapped.

Sea snake acts much faster than other snake venom. This is due to the snakes themselves needing to kill fish quickly, as if the venom were slower the fish would be able to swim much farther than the snake could be able to follow.
