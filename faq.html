FAQs

Here are some of the most common questions we receive from our messages. For an easier time browsing, consult the list below to instantly jump to the answer!

  1. What are the laws relating to owning a snake in the Philippines?
  2. I want to take care of a snake but I don't know anything. Where do I start?
  3. I found a snake near my house! Should I kill it?
  4. Can you identify this snake? Is it dangerous?
  5. Do you rent out snakes for events / photoshoots?

It is perfectly legal to own a snake in the Philippines. Take note of the necessary requirements for the Certificate of Wildlife Acquisition, which would require that the animal in question is acquired from a DENR registered breeder or seller. A Special Local Transport Permit would also be necessary should you need to take your snake elsewhere within the country.

Do note that using a snake (or any other wildlife) for commercial purposes may require additional permits, however such permits are not needed for owning the snake the itself.

First, we recommend checking out the procedure for acquiring a CWR and making sure that you are capable of taking care of a snake.

Snakes are long-term commitments with certain snake species living as long as 30 years. Do some research on the snake specie you are looking to take care of, as different species would have different care requirements. For beginners, we recommend a Ball Python.

No! A significant number of snakebite related injuries occur when people approach a snake and evoke a defensive reaction. By nature, snakes are shy and will naturally avoid humans unless provoked. If the snake is not presenting a threat, it might be better to leave it be. It will help keep your house clean of pests like rats and insects.

If the snake is close enough that you are uncomfortable, we recommend contacting someone with experience handling snakes to remove and relocate it. Do note that taking snakes in the wild for the purpose of selling it is illegal. We also do not recommend keeping it as wild-caught snakes have several problems in adapting to captive life in terms of physical health and stress. Any diseases and parasites it has contacted may spread to your other pets as well.
